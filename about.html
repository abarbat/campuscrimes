About This Project

The information on this website was provided by the FBI's most recent information on US campus crime. Only those universities whose law enforcement agencies have provided the FBI with 12 months of complete offense data for 2014 are represented in the data. Institutions may be excluded from the FBI if their data collection methods deviate from national Uniform Crime Reporting program's guidelines, according to the FBI's data declaration page.

Disclaimers

Misuse of data

This project is not an attempt to rank or compare the safety of schools or states. It was created to present data in an interactive and understandable way. The FBI strongly discourages the use of its data to rank schools for several reasons, which can be reviewed here.

FBI disclaimers

The following items explain the disclaimers inlcuded in the FBI data:

  • The figures are provided by the United States Department of Education. They are for the 2013 school year, which is the most recent available. The figures include full-time and part-time students.
  • Rapes are reported under either the legacy or the revised FBI Uniform Crime Reporting program definition of rape. This is because the definition of rape was redefined by the program. Some agencies began the year submitting rape data classified according to the legacy UCR definition and, at some point in the year, modified reporting methods and began classifying and submitting rape offenses according to the revised definition. The definitions listed by the FBI data declaration page are as follows:
    • Rape (revised definition): Penetration, no matter how slight, of the vagina or anus with any body part or object, or oral penetration by a sex organ of another person, without the consent of the victim. (This includes the offenses of rape, sodomy, and sexual assault with an object as converted from data submitted via the National Incident-Based Reporting System [NIBRS]).
    • Rape (legacy definition): The carnal knowledge of a female forcibly and against her will.
  • Some data was not collected because the FBI did not receive 12 complete months of data from the reporting agency.
  • Student enrollment figures were not available for some schools.
  • Because of changes in the state/local agency's reporting practices, figures for some schools are not comparable to previous years' data. Download the original data here for more detailed information.
  • It is important to rememeber when comparing campuses that campus crime statistics are influenced by factors such as the demographics of the community in which the school is located, the number of residents living on campus versus commuting to school and size.
